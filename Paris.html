Day 28

Monday - 04 Jan 2016

1840 sees us roll into the Paris tired and hungry but supa excited. We are in Paris. We catch the metro 15 min to the hotel and check in. A room change later and we out on the hunt for food. We have some luck as the restaurant across from the hotel has a steak, beans and chips meal. Done. Oh my gawd. They even gook the steak gooooood!

A bit of exploring and some blog work sees us through to bed time.

Day 29

Tuesday - 05 Jan 2016

A terrible night's sleep due to the noise out in the street has us sluggish and uninterested in touristing when the alarm goes off. We snooze till the last possible moment before we have to go down to the included (yes. It is actually included this time. I double checked) breakfast. After a sombre meal we climb back into bed and have another sleep.

It is amazing what a good sleep can do for your mood. By 1300 we are up and ready to tackle Paris. We head towards the river and find the Paris Bourse (Bourse de Paris or Palais Brongniart in French) which was a 19th-century stock exchange, now a conference venue.

Our next discovery is the 17th-century The Royal Place (Le Palais Royal) & gardens with arcades of shops and the Daniel Buren's striped columns in the courtyard.

From here it is only a block to the Louvre. I didn't know this. I didn't actually really have much of a clue where we were. Only that we were still heading vaguely in the direction of the River. Now, Lang who has been to Paris a couple of times knew where we were and what was coming up. Cue "expectant face" for him has we cross the road and then BAM!! The Louvre.

We have a day set aside for actually entering the Louvre so we wander around the controversial glass pyramid and then head in the direction of the Arc de Triomphe. As we leave the massive court yard area of the Louvre and cross the road into the Tuileries Garden (Jardin des Tuileries) I get my first look at the Eiffel Tower. *excited face*

Onward passed the Carrousel du Louvre which is another arc just smaller. Down through the gardens until we get to the https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grande_Roue_de_Paris Grande Roue de Paris (Paris Eye) where the area is chock a block with souvenir stores *squee* in we go. Lang and I have developed this morbid obsession of looking at souvenir stores. We don't usually buy anything but the range of items seems to fascinate us.

As we move down the Avenue Champs-Elysees the souvenir stores turn more into established Christmas markets that are still playing Christmas carols. Have you seen the date?!?! These guys are milking Christmas Holiday Season for ALLLLL it's worth!! Bloody hell! If I never hear another Christmas carol again it still maybe too soon. As we walk further towards the Arc the Christmas market vendors change to proper stores and some flash ones at that. Mercedes, Chanel and Louis Vuitton to name a few. We arrived at the Arc with perfect timing as it is not long until dusk. Just the huge stair case to negotiate to get to a breathtaking view. Magic.

After all that walking we decide to take the metro back to the vicinity of the hotel and hunt down some food. I am DYING for a salad. So Vietnamese it is. I stalk a selection of restaurants through trip advisor and off we go. The first 2 are closed but we catch a break on the third one and finally settle in for a lovely meal at 2000.

During our exploring for the restaurant we found several poles, bins and basically anywhere with a flat surface with a BNE sticker on it. *snigger*

With full bellies we waddle the 20 min back to the hotel and have an early night.

***You don't make friends with salad ... but salad can be delicious***
