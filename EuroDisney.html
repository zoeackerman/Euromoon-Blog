Day 33

Saturday - 09 Jan 2016

By 1500 we have been dropped off at Disney Land and the adventure begins. We smash out the Walt Disney Studio section of the park and make plans for how we are going to spend Sunday and Monday.

The evening ends at 2100 after a spectacular light and sound show. I'm all tingly inside :-D

***Try not to sweat the small stuff.***

Day 34

Sunday - 10 Jan 2016

The park doesn't open until 1000 so we get to sleep in and enjoy our breakfast. The previous evening we had done our research and marked the map provided with all the things we wanted to do. We quickly got down to business.

Lang also go to experience my love for Stitch in full CRAZY mode. Every store they had I wanted a photo with the Stitch merchandise that I really really wanted but could not fit in my suitcase. Even after 5 years of dating me i'm not sure he had a clue what he was getting himself into taking me to Disney Land! *insert maniacal laugh*

***Proper preparation prevents long lines!***

Day 35

Monday - 11 Jan 2016

As we arrive at Disney we pull out our map. We have a couple of rides to check off today. The Armageddon SX Experience, Aerosmith Rock & Roll Roller Coaster, Buzz Lightyear Shooter and the Alice in Wonderland Queen of Hearts Labyrinth.

Let the fun begin!!

The rest of the late afternoon is spent trawling the souvenir shops trying to decide what to get. My list of items is far too long to not only purchase but bring back to Australia. Several hours are spent painstakingly working the list down to something resembling realistic.

We leave the park at about 1800 and take our loot back to the room before heading out to a boutique burger place for dinner.

The rest of the evening is spent packing for the last leg of our adventure. Hong Kong.

******

Day 36

Tuesday - 12 Jan 2016

Lang wakes me as I have slept straight through the alarm. SHIT!! *panic mode engaged*

He promptly reassures me that we are fine and it is only 10 minutes after the alarm. DEFCON 4 aborted.

The left overs quickly eaten for breakfast including the best coconut yogurt I've ever had and we scutter downstairs to find our transfer arriving promptly.

The trip to the airport is a nervous one as the driver has advised that he has to take an alternative route due to a nasty accident on the motorway. Nerves soon pass on to relief as we arrive at the airport exactly as scheduled.

The next couple of hours pass uneventfully clutching the $10 hot chocolate from Starbucks and using their free WIFI. Eventually it is time to board and aside from someone in our seats the flight is relatively uneventful. I do get to catch up on a couple of movies.

***Seat allocation. It isn't optional.***
