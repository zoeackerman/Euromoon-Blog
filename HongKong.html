Day 37

Wednesday - 13 Jan 2016

We touch down in Hong Kong at 0620 and have an uninteresting transition through customs and on to our waiting transfer.

First up is a shower and then head out to explore the local area. We opt for an early lunch and after another hour or so of exploring I am struggling to keep our eyes open so opt to have a nap.

Feeling a bit more refreshed we head back to the markets that we saw earlier in the day. So many goodies!!

Some dinner and a foot massage later and we both flop into bed.

***Foot massages and broken toes don't mix***

Day 38

Thursday - 14 Jan 2016

By 0530 we are wide awake thanks jet lag. Though, it could have been far worse. By the time we both potter in and out of the bathroom and catch up on YouTwitFace it is a reasonable hour so we head out to hunt and gather.

Unfortunately we run into the same problem as in London. Nothing in open. We're hungry enough that when we find an open Maccas we pounce on that sucker.

To my amusement a Hong Kong Maccas breakfast is rather different. The winner of the WTF award being a bowl of something resembling minestrone soup with a fried egg and piece of ham on top.

I must admit though that I did appreciate the laminated picture cards so all we had to do was flick through them and then point out what we wanted to the cashier! Win!!

With full tummies we trek to the ferry for our ride across to the main island. We are going to check out "The Peak".

It is about 1.5km walk to the ferry terminal where we will go by boat to the main island. On the way is a garden full of people doing what looks like tai chi and is decorated with enormous status that looked like they belong in an episode of Dragon Ball Z.

Soon the garden gives way to a flash shopping precinct and then the ferry terminal full of different types of boats. We follow the commuter signs and pay our 0.50 euro for a 1 way token aboard a dinky little ferry.

Our destination is right in the middle of another shopping and entertainment precinct which we explore to the point of getting trapped in fire evacuation stairs. Opps.

Once we freed ourselves our next stop is the base of the Peak Tram where we relax with a hot drink and catch up on some reading before heading to the top.

After the obligatory selfies and info headset we head down to find something for lunch. Unfortunately we chose poorly and had the worst Asian food i've ever had in my life. *sadface*

On returning to sea level we head to the botanic and zoological gardens for a meander. The flamingos are the most amazing colour I have ever seen!

Afterwards we head to the metro back to our island. A very very shinny metro!!

***Hong Kong knows shinny!***
